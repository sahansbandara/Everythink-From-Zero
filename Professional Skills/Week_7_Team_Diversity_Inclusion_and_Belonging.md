### Original Slide Content

- Religion
- It will bring lot of benefits by getting many view points in decision making.
- But this is the most challenging diversity aspect to handle.
- It has to be effectively managed by:
  - Training all employees to educate company policy
  - Be fair in giving time for religious observations
  - Encourage employees to accept differences
  - Avoid overacting to simple things and not to tolerate serious issues

### Visual Explanation

The slide includes an image of religious symbols/figures protected by hands. It communicates that religion needs sensitivity, protection, and respectful management in teams.

### Recreated Management Flow

```text
Religious Diversity
      ↓
Different viewpoints in decision making
      ↓
Possible sensitivity and conflict
      ↓
Manage through policy + fairness + acceptance + serious issue handling
```

### Explanation

Religious diversity can improve decisions because people bring different moral, cultural, and social viewpoints. However, it can also create conflict if employees feel disrespected or if one religion is treated unfairly.

For religious diversity, always write both sides: **benefit = many viewpoints**, **challenge = difficult to handle**, **solution = training, fairness, acceptance, and proper issue handling**.

### Common Mistake

Do not say religious diversity should be ignored at work. It must be managed fairly and respectfully.

## Page 15 — Culture

### Original Slide Content

- Culture refers to the standards of social interaction, value and beliefs from a given group of people.
- Cultural issues can affect team interactions through different understandings of communication or family and can appear to be an excuse for preferential treatment.

### Visual Explanation

No major visual content.

### Definition

Culture is the standards of social interaction, values, and beliefs shared by a group of people.

### Simple Meaning

Culture is how a group usually behaves, communicates, and understands what is normal or important.

### Explanation

Culture affects how people speak, make decisions, respect authority, manage family responsibilities, and interpret behaviour. In teams, cultural differences can create misunderstanding if people do not communicate clearly.

### Visual/Text Diagram

```text
Culture
├── Social interaction standards
├── Values
├── Beliefs
├── Communication style
└── Family / social expectations
```

### Exam Tip

Use examples when explaining culture: communication style, family responsibility, or different expectations about respect and authority.

### Common Mistake

Do not use culture as an excuse for unfair treatment. The slide warns that it can appear as preferential treatment if not managed properly.

## Page 16 — National Culture

### Original Slide Content

- The set of values or beliefs that a society considers important and the norms of behavior that are approved or sanctioned in that society.
- Over time, the culture of a nation changes and this affects the values and beliefs of its members.

### Visual Explanation

No major visual content.

### Definition

National culture is the set of values, beliefs, and accepted behaviour patterns that a society considers important.

### Simple Meaning

National culture is what a country or society generally sees as normal, respectful, important, or acceptable.

### Explanation

National culture is not fixed forever. It changes over time due to education, technology, migration, globalization, religion, politics, and generational change.

### Visual/Text Diagram

```text
National Culture
Values + Beliefs + Approved Behaviour
```

Mention that national culture changes over time. This is a strong point for written answers.

### Common Mistake

Do not say every person in one country behaves the same. National culture describes broad patterns, not every individual.

## Page 18 — Language

### Original Slide Content

- Edward T. Hall — American anthropologist
- Perhaps the most visible aspect of culture.
- Whorfian hypothesis — considers language as a major determinant of thinking.
- Low-context cultures — the message is conveyed by the words used.
  - Examples: USA northern states, Canada, German-speaking countries, Scandinavia, Poland
- High-context cultures — use words to convey only a limited part of the message.
  - Examples: USA southern states, China, Arab countries, Italy, Greece, Japan, Spain, Korea, India, Brazil, Russia
- Video — Low and High Context Cultures

### Visual Explanation

No major visual content. The slide is concept-heavy and compares low-context and high-context cultures.

### Recreated Comparison Table

| Type | Meaning | Message Style | Examples from Slide |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low-context culture | Meaning is mostly in direct words | Explicit and direct | USA northern states, Canada, German-speaking countries, Scandinavia, Poland |
| High-context culture | Words carry only part of the meaning | Indirect; context, relationship, and shared understanding matter | USA southern states, China, Arab countries, Italy, Greece, Japan, Spain, Korea, India, Brazil, Russia |

### Explanation

Language affects thinking and communication. In low-context cultures, people expect direct words. In high-context cultures, meaning may come from tone, relationship, history, body language, and shared cultural knowledge.

### Visual/Text Diagram

```text
Communication Meaning
├── Low-context: words carry most meaning
└── High-context: words + context + relationship carry meaning
```

### Exam Tip

For comparison questions, clearly contrast **direct words** vs **context-based meaning**.

### Common Mistake

Do not say high-context means “bad communication.” It means communication depends more on shared context.

## Page 30 — What is Stereotyping?

### Original Slide Content

- Stereotyping is defined as an “oversimplified, usually pejorative, attitude people hold toward those outside one's own experience who are different.”
- A stereotype is simply a widely held belief that an individual is a member of a certain group based on characteristics.

- Stereotypes are results of selectivity in social perception: We tend to see what we expect to see.

# Likely Exam Questions and Short Answers

## Question 1
Define team diversity.

**Expected Answer:**
Team diversity refers to the differences between individual members of a team, such as age, nationality, religious background, skills, gender, race, personality, and beliefs.

## Question 2
Explain the difference between diversity, inclusion, and belonging.

**Expected Answer:**
Diversity means different people are present in the team. Inclusion means those people are respected, valued, and given equal opportunity to contribute. Belonging is the emotional result where people feel safe, accepted, and valued.

## Question 3
List three benefits of gender diversity.

**Expected Answer:**
Gender diversity widens the talent pool, brings multiple perspectives, improves collaboration, improves staff retention, helps understand customer needs, and improves team or organizational reputation.

## Question 4
Why is religious diversity difficult to manage?

**Expected Answer:**
Religious diversity can involve sensitive beliefs and practices. It must be managed through policy education, fairness in religious observations, acceptance of differences, and proper response to serious issues.

## Question 5
Compare old workers and young workers in an age-diverse team.

**Expected Answer:**
Older workers may bring dedication, punctuality, honesty, maturity, organizational skills, and communication skills. Young workers may bring fresh perspectives, technology adoption, training readiness, affordability, adaptability, and agility.

## Question 6
What is national culture?

**Expected Answer:**
National culture is the set of values, beliefs, and approved behaviour patterns that a society considers important. It changes over time and affects the values and beliefs of its members.

## Question 7
Compare low-context and high-context cultures.

**Expected Answer:**
In low-context cultures, meaning is mainly conveyed by direct words. In high-context cultures, words carry only part of the message and meaning depends on context, relationships, and shared cultural understanding.

## Question 8
What is proxemics?

**Expected Answer:**
Proxemics is the study of how people use physical space to communicate. It reveals cultural differences in personal space, seating, distance, and spatial arrangements.

## Question 9
List five benefits of diversity in the workplace.

**Expected Answer:**
Benefits include a wider range of talents, skills and experiences, more innovation, language skills, a larger talent pool, and improved employee performance.

## Question 10
List five challenges of diversity in the workplace.

**Expected Answer:**
Challenges include discrimination, stereotyping, high turnover, absenteeism, conflict among team members, increased tension, and decreased productivity.

## Question 11
Define discrimination.

**Expected Answer:**
Discrimination means treating a person unfairly because of who they are or because they possess certain characteristics such as age, gender, race, disability, religion, pregnancy/maternity, sexual orientation, gender reassignment, or marriage/civil partnership.

## Question 12
What is stereotyping?

**Expected Answer:**
Stereotyping is an oversimplified attitude or widely held belief about an individual based on group characteristics. It often comes from selective perception and prejudice.

## Question 13
How can managers overcome diversity issues?

**Expected Answer:**
Managers can overcome diversity issues through planning and implementation of a diversity plan, conflict resolution skills, and good communication that encourages employees to share concerns and feel equally important.

## Question 14
How does inclusion support team models?

**Expected Answer:**
Inclusion accelerates Tuckman’s Norming and Performing stages, allows Belbin roles to thrive, improves collaboration, reduces conflict, builds psychological safety, and enhances trust.

## Question 15
Draw a simple diagram showing the relationship between inclusion and belonging.

**Expected Answer:**

```text
Inclusion practices
      ↓
Respect + equal contribution
      ↓
Belonging
      ↓
Trust + psychological safety
```

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Writing that diversity, inclusion, and belonging are the same.
- Defining diversity only as race or gender.
- Saying diversity automatically improves performance without management.
- Ignoring challenges such as discrimination and stereotyping.
- Describing culture as only religion.
- Confusing low-context culture with high-context culture.
- Assuming personal-space rules are the same in every culture.
- Saying discrimination is only about race.
- Treating stereotypes as facts.
- Saying inclusion means simply adding people to a team.
- Forgetting that belonging is emotional and linked to psychological safety.
- Discussing Tuckman or Belbin without linking them to inclusion and team performance.
